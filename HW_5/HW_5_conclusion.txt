At the base 1x scaling, the CPU is faster at this level because the payload is too small to overcome the GPU's OpenMP kernel launch overhead.
But, as the scaling increases (especially at 8x, 16x) the GPU is much faster than the CPU.
This is because the GPU's multiprocessors and high-bandwith memory are fully saturated, and bypasses the CPU memory bandwith bottleneck easily, so the GPU is faster and stays at a much slower incline in runtime speed than the CPU which would get much slower and slower.
